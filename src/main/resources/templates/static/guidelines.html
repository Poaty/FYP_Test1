Community guidelines

This platform is built around honest, emotional reactions to films — not performance, not consensus, not popularity. Some norms that follow from that.

Be honest, not performative

Say what you actually thought. Disagreeing with a popular take is welcome — that's the whole reason this platform exists. Posting an opinion you don't hold because it'll be liked is the thing the platform is designed to resist. There are no like counts here on purpose.

Disagree with takes, not people

Argue with the opinion. Don't attack the person. "I disagree because…" is fine. "You're stupid for thinking that" is not.

No hate speech or harassment

Targeting people based on race, gender, religion, sexuality, disability, nationality, or anything similar is grounds for immediate removal of the content and possibly the account. We don't host this.

No spam or self-promotion

Posts and comments should be about the films themselves. No links to external products, services, or content unless directly relevant to the film being discussed.

Spoilers

If you're discussing plot points beyond the trailer, start the post or comment with spoiler: so people can choose to read on.

A note on threading

You can reply to a top-level comment, but not to a reply. Keeping threads shallow keeps focus on the original post and prevents the pile-on dynamics deeper nesting tends to create. This is a deliberate design choice, not an oversight.

If you see something

Reporting and admin moderation are part of the next iteration of the platform — for now, email the project author at N1205202@my.ntu.ac.uk if a post or comment crosses a line and we'll handle it.
